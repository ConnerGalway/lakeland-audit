01 / EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

What the data tells us.

Travel Lakeland's digital ecosystem is producing strong signals across multiple channels, but those signals are not yet connected into a coordinated strategy. The website has a genuine anchor in the Iron Horse Trail. Organic search delivers high-quality visitors. The email newsletter was performing at an exceptional level before it was discontinued. Instagram engagement on core content themes consistently outperforms Facebook. The audit that follows maps where the evidence leads, and where the measurement gaps prevent confident investment.

This audit incorporates AI-assisted analysis using Perplexity and Claude (Anthropic) to synthesize channel performance data across the website, social, blog, and email channels. All findings and analysis were presented to a senior consultant for review, then validated and incorporated as appropriate. AI tools accelerated pattern recognition and data synthesis; every conclusion reflects Junction's professional judgment.

What's Working
  • Iron Horse Trail: 8,435 page views, 69s avg engagement (site anchor)
  • Organic search: 62.4% engagement rate, 3× longer than Direct traffic
  • Email newsletter: 51.9% open rate in final 6 months, discontinued while performing
  • Operator Feature posts: highest total Facebook clicks, highest Instagram engagement rate
  • Instagram carousels: 7.0% avg engagement vs. 4.3% for single photos
  • Fishing giveaway post: 2,470 organic reach, the largest single-post reach in the period
What's Not Working
  • Conversion tracking not configured in GA4; operator referral value cannot be demonstrated
  • Blog, Operator Feature, Curated Adventure perform 4–5× better on Instagram, yet the strategy is Facebook-first
  • 86% of Facebook posts are photos, despite video generating 2× the engagement rate
  • Sub-brand social channels (IHT, Kalyna Country) near-dormant despite strong organic demand
  • Email newsletter discontinued despite 51.9% open rate, the highest-performing owned channel
  • 870+ malformed tel: URL variants creating dead-end page paths in GA4

Your Six Questions, Answered

What is working well across your digital presence?
Iron Horse Trail, organic search quality, email engagement rate, and Instagram carousel format are the clearest strengths across the ecosystem.
What is underperforming, and why?
Facebook reach for editorial content, sub-brand social channels, and the blog-to-social publishing workflow — each underperforming due to format mismatch and workflow gaps, not content quality.
What is currently driving traffic to the website?
Organic search at 62.4% engagement rate, and direct traffic. Iron Horse Trail is the single strongest content anchor at 8,435 annual views.
Where are the under-appreciated opportunities?
The email newsletter (discontinued while outperforming), Instagram as the primary platform for editorial content, and fishing/dark sky/provincial parks as content gaps with demonstrated search demand.
How should internal resources be structured for greater effectiveness?
A documented publish-to-social workflow would unlock most of the opportunity at no additional cost. Sub-brand channels require a staffing decision — the half-active state is the worst option.
When ready to invest in paid campaigns, where should that focus go?
Not yet. Paid investment cannot be confidently attributed without GA4 conversion tracking in place. Complete Recommendations 1–2 first.

Conversion Tracking Gap
Key events not configured in GA4. 187 operator outbound clicks recorded in just 2 months is a significant under-count and likely represents a fraction of actual referrals being made. Without conversion tracking, every other finding in this report is difficult to act on with confidence.

Technical Issues Requiring Attention

870+ Malformed tel: URL Variants
Phone numbers are generating dead-end navigable page paths in GA4. Each unique malformed variant appears as a separate page, fragmenting data and creating user experience friction when tapped on mobile.
Admin Pages Appearing in GA4 Visitor Data
Login (143 views), dashboard (95 views), and admin (70 views) pages are appearing in standard visitor reports, inflating session counts and skewing engagement metrics.
/submit-event/ Anomaly
247 views with a 2,015-second average engagement time, a pattern consistent with a bot loop. This single page is materially distorting site-wide average engagement calculations.
/mec-external-116/ Redirect
689 views, the 4th most-viewed page on the site, with 0.6-second average engagement. Users are landing and immediately leaving; this redirect is generating significant wasted traffic.
03 / ACQUISITION

Volume ≠ quality. Organic search is your best channel.

Direct traffic is the largest channel by session count, but its 23.7% engagement rate and 19.6-second average engagement time indicate a high proportion of returning visitors, bots, or misdirected traffic. Organic Search delivers visitors who stay 3× longer and engage at nearly 3× the rate, the profile of a trip-planner actively researching the region.

What the content data shows
Sends built around named experiences consistently outperformed broader theme newsletters. The April 2024 send (18 Things to Do) achieved a 10.12% click rate. The March 2025 send (OHV Rides feature) achieved 4.76%. Named destination content (concrete, actionable, experience-first) is the format that drives clicks.
The list decline is administrative, not a performance story
A single April 2024 send recorded 43 unsubscribes (7.25%, more than 10× the typical rate), accounting for the majority of total list loss over the period. After that event, open rates remained above 44% for 14 consecutive months. The list didn't erode from poor performance; it experienced one administrative spike and then continued to perform at exceptional levels.
What restarting looks like
The April 2026 Reactivation Request (745 contacts, 33.7% open rate, 11.95% click rate) confirms the audience is still engaged and the list has actually grown. A quarterly newsletter aligned to seasonal peaks (winter activities in November, spring/summer launch in April, fall events in September) would rebuild a proven owned channel without requiring full monthly production cadence. Add UTM-tagged links from the first send to establish a measurement baseline.
07 / RECOMMENDATIONS

Ten actions, ranked by evidence strength.

Recommendations 1–2 are immediate technical actions that make everything else measurable. Recommendation 3 is a strategic decision with a strong evidence case. From 4 onwards: content and channel investments that can run in parallel.

Set up key events for operator link clicks, newsletter signups, contact form submissions, and Add to Calendar actions. Audit all operator pages for consistent outbound link tagging with UTM parameters. 187 operator outbound clicks recorded in just 2 months is a significant under-count. This action makes every other finding in this report actionable with real measurement behind it.

Investigate /submit-event/ (bot traffic pattern, 2,015s avg engagement time) and /mec-external-116/ (689 sessions, 0.6s engagement). Fix the tel: URL rendering issue creating 870+ malformed page paths in GA4. Exclude or filter admin pages (/login, /dashboard, /admin) from standard GA4 reporting views. These three items are collectively distorting site-wide metrics.

The newsletter averaged 51.9% open rate in its final six months and was discontinued without a performance-based reason. The April 2026 Reactivation Request (33.7% open rate, 11.95% click rate, 745 contacts) confirms the audience remains engaged and the list has grown. Quarterly sends aligned to seasonal peaks (winter activities, spring/summer launch, fall events) would rebuild a proven owned channel without requiring the full monthly production cadence.

Blog posts average 6.9% and Operator Features 7.3% weighted engagement on Instagram, 4–5× their Facebook performance. Carousels average 7.0% vs. 4.3% for single images. Instagram should be the primary channel for these content types; Facebook continues to lead on Events and community content. This is a sequencing shift, not a volume increase: the same content, distributed in priority order.

Facebook videos average 32% more reach (404 vs. 270) and 2× the engagement rate (5.5% vs. 2.8%) compared to photos. Blog, Operator Feature, Curated Adventure, and Signature Experience posts have zero video content on Facebook. Even 2 short-form video posts per month in these themes would begin testing the format advantage for editorial content, with minimal production overhead using existing photo and video assets.

IHT had 1 Instagram post in 5 months and reached 51 people, while its website ecosystem generates 8,435 annual views, the highest of any destination on the site. A 3-posts-per-week calendar covering trail sections, seasonal conditions, and community stops would begin building a social audience to match the organic search demand already established. The audience intent is proven. The social presence simply doesn't exist.

Blog content is the #1 Instagram theme by engagement rate (6.9%). Each new post should trigger: an Instagram carousel feature in the same publication week, a Facebook post, and once the newsletter restarts, an email feature. The publish-to-social sequence can begin immediately with no additional resource. It requires only a documented workflow and editorial discipline. The highest-performing Instagram posts this period were blog carousels at 17.5% and 15.6%.

Event pages already attract significant qualified traffic: Smoky Lake Pumpkin Fair (378 sessions), Cold Lake Ice Fishing Tournament (199 sessions), Opening Ceremonies (659 views). Adding accommodation recommendations, dining options, nearby attractions, and operator links would convert event-intent visitors into broader destination explorers, extending the value of traffic already arriving.

Priority themes: fishing in Lakeland, provincial park camping, dark sky/stargazing, Indigenous cultural experiences, Ukrainian/French-Canadian heritage touring, and snowmobiling. Each post should include a minimum of two operator links and be coordinated with an Instagram carousel and email feature in publication week. These are the themes with proven search demand and zero existing content coverage.

IHT Facebook averages 527 reach per post (2× the Travel Lakeland average). IHT Instagram had 1 post in 5 months. Kalyna Country Facebook had 2 posts with 25 combined reach. Assign dedicated content production at minimum viable volume, or formally consolidate under Travel Lakeland primary accounts with sub-brand tagging. The half-active state serves neither brand nor audience and creates the impression of abandonment to anyone who finds the accounts.

A NOTE ON WHAT COMES NEXT

This audit reveals not just what to fix, but why a coordinated strategy matters.

These findings are operational. Fix the measurement infrastructure. Shift the platform balance. Restart the newsletter. Each action stands on its own and can begin immediately. That's the purpose of this engagement: clear, evidence-based direction you can act on.

What the data also reveals, however, is the absence of a connective strategy. Each channel is producing signals (some strong, some weak) but they're not yet amplifying each other. The IHT content that earns 8,435 annual page views has a near-absent Instagram presence. The email newsletter that was performing exceptionally has no successor. Blog content that outperforms every other Instagram theme gets published without a coordinated social and email sequence.

A strategy engagement would take these findings as its starting point and build the architecture to connect them: seasonal campaign planning, cross-channel workflows, operator partnerships, a paid media roadmap, and an internal capacity model. The data is already telling you where to go.
